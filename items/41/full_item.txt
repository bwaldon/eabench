Query:

"A parent of a 10th-grader has sent us a written request to come in and look through their child's complete education file. Our registrar wants to know: can we require the parent to explain why they want the records, and can we tell them to wait until the end of the grading period when the office is less busy?"

Statutory provision

20 U.S.C. § 1232g(a)(1)(A) No funds shall be made available under any applicable program to any educational agency or institution which has a policy of denying, or which effectively prevents, the parents of students who are or have been in attendance at a school of such agency or at such institution, as the case may be, the right to inspect and review the education records of their children. If any material or document in the education record of a student includes information on more than one student, the parents of one of such students shall have the right to inspect and review only such part of such material or document as relates to such student or to be informed of the specific information contained in such part of such material. Each educational agency or institution shall establish appropriate procedures for the granting of a request by parents for access to the education records of their children within a reasonable period of time, but in no case more than forty-five days after the request has been made.

20 U.S.C. § 1232g(d) For the purposes of this section, whenever a student has attained eighteen years of age, or is attending an institution of postsecondary education, the permission or consent required of and the rights accorded to the parents of the student shall thereafter only be required of and accorded to the student.

Expected follow-up question:

Is the parent asking to inspect and review the education records the school maintains on their own child — a right the school must honor — or asking the school to produce copies, or to see material that also contains information about other students? And has the student's age or enrollment transferred FERPA rights away from the parent?

FERPA guarantees parents the right to inspect and review their child's education records; the school must establish procedures granting access within a reasonable time and in no case more than 45 days after the request, and the right is not conditioned on the parent stating a reason. Where a record includes information on more than one student, the parent may inspect only the portion relating to their own child, or be informed of the specific information about their child. For a 10th-grader who is under 18 and not attending a postsecondary institution, the parent still holds the FERPA rights.

Query Continuation

"The parent gave no reason and does not want copies — they just want to come in and read the file. One document in the file, a report of a cafeteria altercation, names another student as well. The child is 15 and attends only our high school."

Expected Final Answer

The school must let the parent inspect and review the records, and may not condition that on the parent explaining why. Under § 1232g(a)(1)(A), the school must grant access within a reasonable period and no later than 45 days after the request — so telling the parent to wait indefinitely until the office is "less busy," or past the 45-day ceiling, is not permitted; the school may, however, set a specific appointment within that window. The school is not obligated to hand over copies unless failing to do so would effectively prevent the parent from inspecting the records (for example, if distance made an in-person visit impractical), which is not the case here. For the cafeteria-altercation report that also identifies another student, the parent's right reaches only the part of the document relating to their own child: the school may redact or withhold the other student's information, or instead inform the parent of the specific information contained in that part, rather than disclose the whole document. Because the student is 15 and enrolled only at the high school, FERPA rights remain with the parent — they have not transferred to the student under § 1232g(d).

Concepts in explanation

- 45-DAY LIMIT: Access to inspect and review must be granted within a reasonable time, and in no case more than 45 days after the request.
- NO REASON REQUIRED: The right to inspect and review is not conditioned on the parent justifying the request.
- MULTI-STUDENT REDACTION: Where a record covers more than one student, the parent may inspect only the portion relating to their own child, or be informed of that portion's specific contents.
