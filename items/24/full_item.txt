Relevant Provision
(b)(4)(A) Each educational agency or institution shall maintain a record, kept with the education records of each student, which will indicate all individuals (other than those specified in paragraph (1)(A) of this subsection), agencies, or organizations which have requested or obtained access to a student’s education records maintained by such educational agency or institution, and which will indicate specifically the legitimate interest that each such person, agency, or organization has in obtaining this information. Such record of access shall be available only to parents, to the school official and his assistants who are responsible for the custody of such records, and to persons or organizations authorized in, and under the conditions of, clauses (A) and (C) of paragraph (1) as a means of auditing the operation of the system.
(1) No funds shall be made available under any applicable program to any educational agency or institution which has a policy or practice of permitting the release of education records (or personally identifiable information contained therein other than directory information, as defined in paragraph (5) of subsection (a)) of students without the written consent of their parents to any individual, agency, or organization, other than to the following—(A) other school officials, including teachers within the educational institution or local educational agency, who have been determined by such agency or institution to have legitimate educational interests, including the educational interests of the child for whom consent would otherwise be required;
Query
I am the data privacy officer for a mid-sized school district. We have never maintained a centralized log of who has accessed individual students’ records. A parent has now asked for a list of every person and organization that has had access to her child’s education records over the past three years. Are we obligated to provide this?
Expected follow-up question:
Of the disclosures you describe, which were made to school officials (including teachers) within the district who have legitimate educational interests in the child, and which were made to outside parties such as transferring schools, subpoena recipients, or state auditors?
Query Continuation
The disclosures the parent is asking about are to outside parties. 
Expected Final Answer
You are likely out of compliance with FERPA’s recordkeeping obligation under (b)(4)(A). FERPA requires the district to maintain, with each student’s education records, a record of every individual, agency, or organization that has requested or obtained access to that student’s records, together with the specific legitimate interest of each requester. The only category of disclosures that is exempted from this logging requirement is disclosures to school officials within the institution who have legitimate educational interests under (b)(1)(A).
One narrow exception to bear in mind: under § 1232g(j)(4), records produced under a terrorism-investigation ex parte order are not subject to (b)(4) recordkeeping; if your district ever receives such an order, the disclosure must not be logged.
