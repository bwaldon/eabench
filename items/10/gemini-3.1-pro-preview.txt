**1. The Mandatory Five-Year Ban**
Your primary legal obligation under FERPA in this specific scenario is found in **subsection (b)(4)(B)**. 

When you initially shared the records with the nonprofit, you likely did so under the "studies exception" outlined in **subsection (b)(1)(F)**, which allows districts to share data for the purpose of "improving instruction." However, subsection (b)(4)(B) explicitly states that personal information can only be transferred to a third party on the condition that they *will not permit any other party to have access* to it without the written consent of the parents. 

Because the nonprofit (the third party) improperly redisclosed the data to the technology company in violation of the parental consent requirements of paragraph (2)(A), FERPA mandates a strict penalty: **Your school district is now legally prohibited from permitting that nonprofit organization access to any information from education records for a period of not less than five years.**

While you must immediately enforce the five-year ban, you should also demand that the nonprofit and the technology company immediately destroy all personally identifiable student data they possess to comply with the statutory requirement that the data be destroyed when the original instructional study concluded.
