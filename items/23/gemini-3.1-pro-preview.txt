As general counsel, you are dealing with a highly sensitive and specific exception to standard FERPA rules. Based on the provided text of the statute, here is the legal analysis regarding your obligations.

**1. Must the university comply with the ex parte order?**
**Yes.** You must comply with the order, and the university is legally protected in doing so. 

Under **Subsection (j)(1)** (Investigation and Prosecution of Terrorism), the Attorney General or a designated federal officer may apply for an *ex parte* court order requiring an educational institution to permit the collection of education records relevant to an authorized investigation or prosecution of specific terrorism offenses. 

Furthermore, you have a safe harbor. Under **Subsection (j)(3)**, an educational agency or institution that produces education records in good faith in accordance with an order issued under this subsection *"shall not be liable to any person for that production."*

**2. What are your obligations toward the student?**
Counter-intuitively to standard FERPA practices, **you have absolutely no obligation to notify the student, nor should you record this disclosure in the student's file.** 

Here is how the statute alters your standard obligations in this specific scenario:

*   **No Advance Notice to the Student:** Under normal circumstances, Subsection (b)(2)(B) requires an institution to notify the student (since they are in postsecondary education, per Subsection (d)) *in advance* of complying with a judicial order or subpoena. However, **Subsection (j)(1)** explicitly begins with the phrase: *"Notwithstanding subsections (a) through (i) or any provision of State law..."* This overrides the standard notification requirements. Because it is an *ex parte* order (meaning it is granted without the other party's knowledge), notifying the student would undermine the DOJ's terrorism investigation and is not required by FERPA.
*   **No Record-Keeping Requirement:** Normally, under Subsection (b)(4)(A), the university must maintain a log within the student's education records detailing any individual or agency that requested or obtained access to the records. However, **Subsection (j)(4)** explicitly states: *"Subsection (b)(4) does not apply to education records subject to a court order under this subsection."*
