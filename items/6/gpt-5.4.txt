Yes—potentially, but only if your school has properly designated that information as “directory information” under FERPA and given parents/eligible students a chance to opt out.

Key FERPA points:

- “Directory information” can include a student’s:
  - name
  - participation in officially recognized activities and sports
  - weight and height of members of athletic teams
- FERPA allows release of directory information without prior written consent only if:
  1. the school has given public notice of what it designates as directory information, and
  2. parents (or eligible students) were given a reasonable opportunity to opt out, and
  3. the particular student has not opted out.

For your specific request:
- Student athletes’ names: can be directory information.
- The sports they play: generally falls within “participation in officially recognized activities and sports,” so this also can be directory information.

So, you may share the list with the newspaper if:
- your district/school’s annual FERPA notice designates these items as directory information, and
- none of the listed students have opted out of directory information disclosures.

Do not disclose the information if:
- your FERPA notice does not include student name and/or participation in sports as directory information, or
- a parent/eligible student opted out.

Practical step:
- Check your district’s annual FERPA directory information notice and your opt-out records before responding.

A safe response rule:
- If both are covered and no opt-out exists → yes, you may disclose.
- If not → no, you should not disclose without consent.
