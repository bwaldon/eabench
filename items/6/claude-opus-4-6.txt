# Sharing Student-Athlete Names and Sports Under FERPA

## Short Answer

**Yes, you may share this information — but only if you have followed the required procedural steps.** Student names and participation in officially recognized activities and sports both fall squarely within FERPA's definition of **"directory information"** under **subsection (a)(5)(A)**.

---

## Legal Basis

Subsection (a)(5)(A) explicitly defines directory information to include, among other things:

> *"the student's name, address, telephone listing, date and place of birth, major field of study, **participation in officially recognized activities and sports**, weight and height of members of athletic teams, dates of attendance, degrees and awards received..."*

Because directory information is **carved out** from the general prohibition on releasing education records without parental consent — as stated in subsection (b)(1) — it may be disclosed to third parties, including a newspaper, **provided the conditions in subsection (a)(5)(B) have been satisfied**.

---

## Mandatory Prerequisites Before Disclosure

Under **subsection (a)(5)(B)**, you must ensure **both** of the following:

1. **Public Notice** — Your school must have given **public notice** of the specific categories of information it has designated as directory information for students attending the school.

2. **Opt-Out Period** — You must have allowed **a reasonable period of time** after that notice for any parent to inform the school that any or all of the designated directory information **should not be released** without the parent's prior consent.

---

## Practical Steps You Should Take

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1** | Confirm that your school's annual FERPA notice **explicitly designates** student names and sports participation as directory information. |
| **2** | Verify that the required opt-out period has passed for the current school year. |
| **3** | Check your records for **any students or parents who have opted out** of directory information disclosure. |
| **4** | **Remove from the list** any student whose parent (or the student, if 18+) has exercised the opt-out right. |
| **5** | Only then release the remaining names and sports to the newspaper. |

---

## Key Cautions

- **Opt-outs are absolute.** If even one parent has opted out, that student's name must be withheld — there is no journalistic or public-interest exception that overrides the opt-out.
- **Do not disclose beyond what is designated.** If the newspaper also asks for information you have *not* designated as directory information (e.g., GPAs, disciplinary records), that falls under the general consent requirement of subsection (b)(1) and **cannot** be shared without written parental consent.
- **Students aged 18 or older** hold these rights themselves under **subsection (d)**, so their own opt-out decisions, rather than their parents', control.

---

## Bottom Line

The information the newspaper is requesting is precisely the type FERPA contemplates being publicly releasable as directory information. Your obligation is to **ensure the notice-and-opt-out process has been properly completed** and to **honor every opt-out** before providing the list.
