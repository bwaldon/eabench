Query:

"A prospective employer called our registrar's office asking us to confirm a recent graduate's GPA and to send over a copy of their transcript for a background check. They said the applicant listed our college on their résumé. Can we send it?"

Statutory provision

20 U.S.C. § 1232g(b)(1) No funds shall be made available under any applicable program to any educational agency or institution which has a policy or practice of permitting the release of education records (or personally identifiable information contained therein other than directory information, as defined in paragraph (5) of subsection (a)) of students without the written consent of their parents to any individual, agency, or organization, other than to the following [enumerated exceptions].

20 U.S.C. § 1232g(b)(2)(A) No funds shall be made available … to any educational agency or institution which has a policy or practice of releasing … personally identifiable information in education records other than directory information … unless there is written consent from the student's parents specifying records to be released, the reasons for such release, and to whom, and with a copy of the records to be released to the student's parents and the student if desired by the parents.

20 U.S.C. § 1232g(a)(5)(A) For the purposes of this section the term "directory information" relating to a student includes the following: the student's name, address, telephone listing, date and place of birth, major field of study, participation in officially recognized activities and sports, weight and height of members of athletic teams, dates of attendance, degrees and awards received, and the most recent previous educational agency or institution attended by the student.

Expected follow-up question:

Two things: (1) Is the specific information requested — GPA and a full transcript — "directory information" that the institution has designated and publicly noticed, or is it non-directory personally identifiable information (grades are)? (2) Has the student provided prior written consent that specifies these records, the purpose, and this employer as the recipient — noting that merely listing the school on a résumé is not such consent?

FERPA's baseline rule under § 1232g(b)(1) forbids releasing education records, or personally identifiable information from them other than properly designated directory information, without the prior written consent of the parent or eligible student, unless one of the statute's enumerated exceptions applies. A private prospective employer is not among the (b)(1) exceptions. GPA and transcript grades are not directory information under § 1232g(a)(5)(A). Valid consent under § 1232g(b)(2)(A) must be written and must specify the records to be released, the reason for the release, and the party to whom it is made.

Query Continuation

"There is no consent form on file — the applicant just listed us as their school on the résumé. The employer wants the actual GPA and transcript, not just confirmation that the person attended and earned a degree."

Expected Final Answer

No — the registrar may not send the GPA or transcript to the employer without the graduate's prior written consent. GPA and transcript grades are personally identifiable information from education records, not directory information, so § 1232g(b)(1)'s consent requirement applies, and none of that subsection's exceptions covers a private employer conducting a background check. Listing the school on a résumé is not consent: § 1232g(b)(2)(A) requires written consent that specifies the records to be released, the reason for the release, and the party to whom it is made. The correct path is to have the applicant provide (or the employer obtain from the applicant) a signed, dated release meeting those requirements; the college may then disclose. Note the narrow exception: dates of attendance and the degree/award received are directory information under § 1232g(a)(5)(A), so if the institution has designated those categories as directory information, given the required public notice, and the graduate did not opt out, the school could confirm enrollment and the degree even without consent — but that does not extend to the GPA or the transcript itself.

Concepts in explanation

- BASELINE CONSENT RULE: Personally identifiable information from education records may not be released without prior written consent unless a § 1232g(b)(1) exception applies.
- CONSENT CONTENT: Valid consent must be written and specify the records, the reason, and the recipient; a résumé listing is not consent.
- DIRECTORY-INFORMATION LINE: Degree and dates of attendance can be directory information; GPA and transcript grades cannot.
