Based on the provided FERPA text, whether you are required to grant the parent's request depends entirely on the **age** and **current educational status** of the former student. 

Here is how FERPA applies to your situation:

**1. If the former student is under 18 years old (and not attending a postsecondary institution)**
**Yes, you must grant the request.** 
Under **Section (a)(1)(A)**, FERPA rights apply to the parents of students who *"are or have been in attendance at a school."* The fact that the child is a *former* student does not void the parent's right to inspect and review the education records. You must establish appropriate procedures to grant this access within a reasonable timeframe, not to exceed **45 days** after the request is made.

**2. If the former student is 18 years of age or older (or is attending a postsecondary institution)**
**No, you are not required to grant the request.** 
Under **Section (d)**, once a student turns 18 years old OR begins attending a postsecondary education institution (e.g., college), all FERPA rights regarding permission, consent, and access automatically transfer from the parent to the student. 

If the rights have transferred to the student, you cannot release the records to the parent without the **written consent of the student** specifying the records to be released, the reasons, and to whom, as outlined in **Section (b)(2)(A)**.

*Exception for Dependent Students:* Even if the student is 18 or older, **Section (b)(1)(H)** permits (but does *not require*) you to release records to the parents without the student's written consent if the student is considered a "dependent student" for tax purposes (as defined in section 152 of title 26/the IRS code). 

**Summary for the Administrator:**
Before releasing any records, you should first verify the former student's age and whether they are currently enrolled in college. 
*   If the student is under 18 and not in college, you must provide the parent access within 45 days. 
*   If the student is 18 or older (or in college), deny the parent's request and require them to either produce the student's written consent or provide proof that the student is a recognized tax dependent.
