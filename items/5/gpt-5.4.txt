Yes—generally, **yes**, if the former student **is or has been in attendance** at your school and the FERPA rights have **not transferred to the student**.

Key FERPA rule from the text you provided:

- Schools that receive federal funds must give **parents of students who are or have been in attendance** the right to **inspect and review education records**.  
- The school must provide access **within a reasonable time, and no later than 45 days** after the request.

But the crucial issue is **who currently holds the FERPA rights**:

### 1. If the former student is **under 18** and **not attending a postsecondary institution**
Then **the parent retains FERPA rights**, even if the student is no longer enrolled at your high school.  
In that case, **yes, you generally must allow the parent to inspect and review the records**.

### 2. If the former student is **18 or older**, or **attending a postsecondary institution**
Then under FERPA, the rights transfer from the parent to the **student**.  
In that case, **the parent does not automatically have a right of access** just because they are the parent.

A parent may still get access if, for example:

- the **eligible student gives written consent**, or
- the parent qualifies under a FERPA exception, such as the student being a **dependent for federal tax purposes**.

### 3. “Former student” does not eliminate FERPA rights
FERPA expressly covers students who **“are or have been in attendance.”** So being a former student does **not** by itself defeat the access right.

### 4. What records are covered
Grades and disciplinary records are typically **education records** under FERPA, subject to limited exceptions.

### Bottom line
You are required to grant the request **if the requesting parent still holds FERPA rights**—most commonly because the former student is **under 18 and not in postsecondary education**.  
If the former student is **18+** or has become a **postsecondary student**, the parent is **not automatically entitled** to inspect the records.

### Practical next step
Before releasing anything, confirm:

- the identity of the requesting parent,
- the former student’s **age**,
- whether the student is now attending a **postsecondary institution**, and
- whether any **court order, custody limitation, or dependency documentation** affects access.
