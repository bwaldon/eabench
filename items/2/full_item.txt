Query:
"We are a state education agency. We use a third-party vendor to operate our student data platform. The vendor's engineers need access to individually identifiable student records to perform maintenance and troubleshoot a technical issue. We do not have parental consent for this access. Are we in compliance with FERPA?"
Relevant Statutory Provision
20 U.S.C. § 1232g(b)(1)(A)
Nothing contained in this section shall be construed to prohibit educational agencies or institutions from disclosing education records to other school officials, including teachers, within the agency or institution determined to have legitimate educational interests, including an educational agency or institution that has contracted with an outside organization for any institutional service or function for which the agency or institution would otherwise use its own employees, provided that such outside organization is subject to the requirements of this section governing use and redisclosure of personally identifiable information from education records.
Expected follow-up question:
What do the SEA's contracts with the vendor say about the vendor's obligations regarding student data, specifically whether the vendor is contractually bound by FERPA requirements equivalent to those applicable to the SEA's own employees?
The school official exception under §1232g(b)(1)(A) permits disclosure to outside contractors only if three conditions are met: the contractor performs an institutional service or function, is under the direct control of the agency with respect to the records, and is subject to the same FERPA conditions as agency employees. The contract terms are the legally determinative fact.
Query Continuation
"Our contract with the vendor designates them as a contractor, prohibits them from using student data for any purpose other than providing services to the SEA, and requires them to comply with FERPA. The contract does not, however, limit which individual engineers within the vendor company can access the data."
Expected Final Answer
The SEA is likely in compliance with FERPA since the vendor contract satisfies the main conditions of the school official exception under §1232g(b)(1)(A): the vendor is performing an institutional service, is contractually bound by FERPA requirements, and is prohibited from using data for other purposes. However, the absence of a contractual limitation on which individual engineers can access the data is a meaningful compliance risk. FERPA's legitimate educational interest requirement implies that access should be limited to those with a genuine need for it in connection with the contracted service. The SEA should consider amending the contract to require the vendor to limit internal access to those engineers directly involved in maintaining or supporting the platform, and to maintain an access log.
