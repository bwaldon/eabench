Yes. Under FERPA, the sending school generally may disclose the student’s education records, including disciplinary records, to your school without prior parental consent if the student seeks or intends to enroll at your school.

Key FERPA basis:
- 20 U.S.C. § 1232g(b)(1)(B): records may be disclosed to “officials of other schools or school systems in which the student seeks or intends to enroll,” provided the parents are notified of the transfer, can get a copy if desired, and have an opportunity for a hearing to challenge the content.
- 20 U.S.C. § 1232g(h)(2): FERPA specifically says nothing prohibits a school from disclosing disciplinary information to teachers and school officials, including those in other schools, who have legitimate educational interests in the student’s behavior.

What that means for your situation:
- If the student is actually transferring, applying, registered, or otherwise intends to enroll in your school, the sending district may formally send the disciplinary records to you.
- A suspension for bringing a weapon to school would ordinarily be part of the student’s education record and can be included in the transfer disclosure.
- You should request the records through normal enrollment/records-transfer channels, not just through informal staff-to-staff conversation.

Important conditions:
- The disclosure should be for a legitimate school purpose tied to enrollment, placement, supervision, and safety planning.
- The sending school must comply with FERPA’s notice requirements unless its annual FERPA notice already says it forwards records to schools where students seek/intend to enroll, which many districts do under the regulations.
- Once your school receives the records, they remain FERPA-protected and should be shared internally only with staff who have a legitimate educational interest.

Practical answer:
- Yes, ask for the records formally.
- Make clear the student is enrolling/intends to enroll and that the records are needed for safe transition and educational planning.
- Limit internal access to those who need to know.
