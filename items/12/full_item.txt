Under FERPA, is the sending school allowed to share those records with us?

Expected response:
Final output: Yes, FERPA permits the sending school to share those disciplinary records with you. FERPA explicitly allows an educational institution to disclose information about disciplinary action taken for conduct that posed a significant risk to safety, to teachers and school officials, including those in other schools, who have legitimate educational interests in the student’s behavior. As the receiving principal responsible for campus safety, you have a clear legitimate educational interest. But there is an important caveat: this provision applies only to disciplinary records involving conduct that posed a “significant risk” to safety. It does not authorize blanket disclosure of the student’s entire disciplinary history, minor infractions that did not rise to that threshold remain subject to FERPA’s ordinary consent requirements.
